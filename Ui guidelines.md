1. Typography & Color (Apple Style)
Apple’s design language (Human Interface Guidelines) focuses on high legibility and "negative space."
Font: Use a clean, sans-serif font. If you are developing for web, Inter or System-UI are the closest free alternatives to Apple's San Francisco.
Text Colors:
Primary: #000000 (Pure Black) or #1D1D1F (Off-black) for headers.
Secondary: #86868B for subtext and descriptions.
Background: Use a pure white background (#FFFFFF) to make the product imagery or text "pop."
Hierarchy: Use bold, large headers (e.g., 40px+) for section titles, similar to the "iPhone" header in your first screenshot.
2. Login Page (Instagram "Lite")
Following your request to strip down the Instagram login while keeping the look:
Layout: Center the login box vertically and horizontally.
Fields: Two input boxes with rounded corners (approx. 3px to 5px radius) and a very light grey border (#DBDBDB).
The Button: A solid blue button (#0095F6) with white text.
Omitted Elements: Do not include the "Forgot Password" link, the Facebook login button, or the bottom "Sign Up" footer.
Brand Placement: Place your logo in the top center using a script font or high-quality PNG, centered above the input fields.
3. Navigation Sidebar (Apple Layout + Kitabay Motion)
You want the structure of an Apple sidebar but the "slide-in" feel of the Kitabay menu.
Structure (Apple Style):
Left-aligned text with generous vertical padding.
Use icons only where necessary (thin, grey line icons).
Group items under headers like "Library," "Settings," or "Genres" in all-caps, light-grey text.
Behavior (Kitabay Style):
Trigger: An "Overlay" transition. When the hamburger menu is clicked, the sidebar should slide in from the left.
Overlay: The rest of the screen should have a subtle dark blur or dimming effect (backdrop-filter) to focus the user on the menu.
Exit: Include a clear "X" close button at the top right of the sidebar, as seen in your second screenshot.
